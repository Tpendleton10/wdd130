White water rafting is an outdoor adventure sport where participants navigate through fast-flowing rivers or streams in an inflatable raft. The river's turbulent waters create rapids, which range in intensity from mild to wild, offering an exciting challenge. Rafting typically involves teamwork, with paddlers working together to steer the raft, avoid obstacles, and navigate through rough water.
